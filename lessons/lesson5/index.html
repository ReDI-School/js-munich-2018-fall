Comparisons ‚öñÔ∏è

  • Comparisons are what we put inside a if(...)
  • Are used to compare two values
  • They result in booleans!
    • 
                        let x = prompt("Please enter the first number");
                        let y = prompt("Please enter the second number");
                        alert(x === y);

  • We can also put the result into a variable:
    • 
                        let x = 12;
                        let y = 12;
                        let z = x === y;
                        alert("z is a " + typeof(z) + " and contains " + z);

Types of comparisons

  • We've seen === (equals)
  • < (smaller than)
  • > (larger than)
  • <= (smaller than or equal to)
  • >= (larger than or equal to)
  • !== (not equal)

Guess the results:

  • alert(12 > 33);
  • alert(12 < 33);
  • alert(12 < 12);
  • alert(12 <= 12);
  • alert(12 === 33);
  • alert(12 !== 33);
  • alert("bla" === "bla");
  • alert("bla" !== "bla");
  • alert("bla" === 12);
  • There are many other ways to create complex comparisons!
  • We will cover them later üòÅ

Logical operators

  • Often logic is too complex for a simple condition
  • Need to combine conditions together
  • and - &&
  • or - ||
  • or - !

Guess the results:

  • alert(2 > 1 && 2 < 3);
  • alert(2 > 1 && 2 > 3);
  • alert(2 > 1 || 2 > 3);
  • alert(!(2 > 1));
